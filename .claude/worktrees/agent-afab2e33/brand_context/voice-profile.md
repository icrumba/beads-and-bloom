# Beads & Bloom — Voice Profile

## Voice Summary
Beads & Bloom sounds like two impressively poised 13-year-olds who make adults think "wow, these girls are amazing." The voice is warm, charming, and genuinely enthusiastic — mature enough to earn respect, young enough to feel refreshingly real. It makes moms and aunts want to support them, share their story, and root for their success. Not teen-to-teen chatter — it's teens who impress adults.

## Core Energy
- **Role:** Charming young entrepreneurs / Inspiring creators
- **Default energy:** Warm confidence that makes adults go "these girls are going places"
- **Recurring themes:** Ocean-inspired design (sea turtles, starfish, seashells, plumeria flowers), giving back, creativity, sisterhood, handmade craft, custom colors, the personal touch (handwritten thank-you notes in every order)

## Personality Traits
- **Impressive:** Mature beyond their years — adults are genuinely surprised and charmed by their professionalism
- **Genuine:** Authentically young without being juvenile — real enthusiasm that adults find endearing, not cringey
- **Caring:** The charity mission and handwritten thank-you notes show character that makes adults want to support them
- **Creative:** Thoughtful design choices — signature ocean charms (sea turtles, starfish, seashells, plumeria), custom color palettes, and pieces that range from fun stacked bracelets to elegant pearl pieces
- **Poised:** Confident in their craft and their mission — not trying too hard, just genuinely good at what they do

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal <> Casual | Casual but polished — charming, not sloppy | Natural teen warmth with enough poise to impress adult buyers |
| Serious <> Playful | Playful with heartfelt moments | Fun and light, but sincere when talking about charity, craft, or mission |
| Reserved <> Bold | Confidently enthusiastic | Proud of their work in a way that makes adults root for them |
| Simple <> Sophisticated | Simple words, impressive execution | Clear language that shows maturity — adults think "wow, they wrote this?" |
| Warm <> Direct | Warm first, direct when needed | Leads with heart, includes signature details (ocean charms, custom options, thank-you notes) |

## Vocabulary

**Use:**
- "handmade with love" — core brand phrase
- "one of a kind" — because no two pieces are exactly alike
- "ocean-inspired" — captures the signature aesthetic
- "giving back" — how they talk about the charity mission
- "sea turtle / starfish / seashell / plumeria" — name the specific charms (signature differentiators)
- "custom colors" — mention that buyers can request their favorite palette
- "handwritten thank-you note" — personal touch that sets them apart
- "drop" — for new collection releases
- emojis: ocean-related and hearts, used sparingly and naturally

**Avoid:**
- "curated" — too corporate
- "artisan" — sounds pretentious for teen founders
- "luxury" — not the vibe, these are fun and accessible
- "utilize" / "leverage" / "synergy" — corporate language
- "soooo" / "bestie" / "omg" overload — a little is fine, but too much reads as teen-to-teen instead of impressive-teens-to-adults
- "shop now" as a cold command — soften to "check it out!" or "grab yours!"
- "influencer" — they're creators, not influencers

**Jargon level:** None — everything should be understandable by an 8-year-old
**Profanity:** None

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Short and confident. Mix of warm openers (3-6 words) and descriptive details (8-15 words) that weave in signature elements — ocean charms, custom colors, handwritten notes. Exclamation marks used with purpose, not in every sentence.
**Paragraphs:** 1-2 sentences on social. 2-3 for product descriptions. Always weave in at least one signature detail per piece.
**Fragments:** Sparingly — works for emphasis ("Every single bead. Hand-picked by us.") but not overused
**Openings:** Lead with something that makes adults take notice. "We just finished something special." / "New collection — and this one's our favorite yet."
**Closers:** Warm call to action tied to the mission or personal touch. "Every bracelet gives back!" / "And yes — every order comes with a handwritten thank-you note from us."
**Formatting:** Emojis used naturally (not stuffed). Line breaks between ideas on social.

## POV & Address

- **Person:** We (the twins as a team)
- **Reader address:** You / "you guys" (sparingly)
- **Relationship stance:** Impressive young creators that adults are proud to support — not peers, not a corporate brand

## Confidence Zones

| Zone | Topics | Sounds like |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Full authority | Their designs, bead selection, ocean theme, handmade process, charity mission | "We hand-pick every single bead!" / "Every order = $1 to charity" |
| Earned perspective | Running a small business as teens, what customers love | "We've learned so much this year!" / "You guys always love the turtle charms" |
| Active exploration | New platforms, growing the business, trying new styles | "We're trying something new!" / "Let us know what you think!" |

## Signature Phrases

**Transitions:**
- "Ok but..." — when about to show something exciting
- "Also!" — adding another fun detail

**Emphasis:**
- "Handmade with love" — core brand tagline
- "One bracelet at a time" — mission statement closer
- "No two are exactly alike" — uniqueness of handmade

**Closers:**
- "Making the world more beautiful — one bracelet at a time"
- "Every purchase gives back!"
- "Made with love by us"

## Platform Adaptations

| Platform | Tone shift | Structure | Length |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Instagram | Most natural home — full personality, visual-first | Short caption + emojis, line breaks, CTA | 1-3 sentences + hashtags |
| Etsy | Slightly more descriptive, still warm | Product details up front, story woven in | 100-200 words per listing |
| TikTok | Most casual, behind-the-scenes energy | Hook in first 2 sec, show the making process | 15-60 second videos |
| Website | Warmest and most complete — tells the full story | Sections: hero, about us, shop, mission | Varies by page |
| Email | Personal, like a letter from the twins | Short paragraphs, one CTA, sign off as "the twins" | 50-150 words |

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use emojis naturally (ocean ones, hearts, sparkles)
- Show the making process — people love seeing the hands-on work
- Mention the charity mission in every major piece of content
- Talk like real teens — contractions, casual grammar, excitement
- Feature the twin dynamic — "we" is stronger than "I"

**DON'T:**
- Sound like an adult wrote it and is pretending to be a teen
- Use corporate marketing language
- Over-polish the grammar — a little casual is authentic
- Forget the mission — the charity angle is what makes people feel good about buying
- Be pushy about sales — excitement about the product sells itself

## Anti-Patterns

Things they'd never say:
- "Elevate your accessory game" — too corporate, too adult
- "Premium handcrafted pieces" — sounds like a luxury brand, not teen creators
- "Limited inventory remaining" — artificial scarcity pressure
- "As seen on..." — they're not chasing clout
- "Our team of artisans" — it's two sisters at their kitchen table
