# Beads & Bloom — Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are

**In one sentence:** Moms, aunts, and gift-givers (ages 25-45) who love supporting young entrepreneurs and meaningful causes — and are also buying for daughters and nieces (ages 8-15) who love trendy, colorful jewelry.

**Situation:** Scrolling Instagram or browsing Etsy looking for gifts or a treat — drawn to handmade, small-batch products with a story behind them. Often buying for both themselves and the young girls in their lives.
**Identity:** "I'm someone who puts thought into what I buy. I'd rather support a small business with a mission than grab something generic off Amazon."
**Role:** Gift-giver, mom, aunt, or self-treating shopper who values meaning behind purchases
**Stage:** Active shoppers who already buy handmade — they know Etsy, they follow small brands on Instagram, and they're always looking for the next find that feels special.

## Primary Pain

**The problem:** Finding jewelry that's affordable, unique, age-appropriate for young girls AND stylish enough for adults — while also feeling good about where the money goes.
**What it costs them:** Time scrolling through hundreds of generic Etsy listings, settling for mass-produced jewelry that doesn't feel special, or overpaying for "artisan" pieces that don't match the vibe they want.
**How they describe it:** "I want something cute and unique but not cheaply made" / "I love supporting small businesses, especially ones with a good cause" / "My daughter wants trendy bracelets but I want them to be quality"

**Secondary pains:**
- Generic jewelry that everyone already has — nothing feels special anymore
- Hard to find gifts that are both affordable and meaningful
- Kids want trendy styles but parents want quality and a good story behind the product

## What They've Tried

| Alternative | Why it didn't work | What they learned |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Amazon / Target jewelry | Mass-produced, no story, breaks easily, everyone has the same thing | They want something that feels personal and unique |
| Other Etsy handmade sellers | Overwhelming choice, inconsistent quality, no emotional connection to the maker | A founder story and mission matter — they want to know who they're supporting |
| Pura Vida / larger brands | Nice concept but lost the "small" feeling — feels more corporate now | They prefer genuinely small, genuinely personal brands |
| DIY bead kits | Fun but time-consuming, results don't look professional | They'd rather buy from someone with real skill and a creative eye |

**Sophistication level:** Intermediate — they've bought handmade before and know what they like. Not first-time Etsy shoppers.

## Desired Outcome

**What "after" looks like:** They found their go-to jewelry brand for gifts and self-treats. They feel good every time they buy because it's handmade, unique, and gives back. Their daughter or niece lights up when she sees the ocean charms. They tell their friends about "these two amazing teen girls who make jewelry and donate to charity."
**What they'd tell a friend:** "You have to check out this brand — it's run by these two 13-year-old twins and every bracelet donates $1 to charity. The jewelry is adorable and totally unique."
**The deeper motivation:** Being a thoughtful gift-giver. Supporting young people doing something meaningful. Teaching the kids in their lives that purchases can have purpose.

## Objections

| Objection | What's really behind it | How to address |
|-----------|------------------------|----------------|
| "Is the quality good enough if they're made by teens?" | Concern about durability and craftsmanship | Show close-up photos of finished pieces, share customer reviews, mention quality materials and personal bead selection |
| "How do I know the charity donation is real?" | Trust — especially with online brands | Be transparent about which charities, share receipts or totals donated, post updates on charity impact |
| "Shipping might take too long if it's handmade" | They need it by a specific date (gift-giving) | Clear shipping timelines on the website, offer rush options if possible, set expectations upfront |

## Their Language

**Words they use for the problem:**
- "I want something unique, not something everyone has" — browsing fatigue
- "I love supporting small businesses" — values-driven shopping
- "My daughter is obsessed with bracelets right now" — buying for young girls

**Words they use for the desired outcome:**
- "So cute!" / "Adorable!" — first reaction to the product
- "I love the story behind this" — emotional connection
- "This is the perfect gift" — gift-giving satisfaction

**Words that would turn them off:**
- "Luxury" — feels out of touch for a teen brand, implies high prices
- "Limited stock" / "Hurry" — pressure tactics feel inauthentic
- "Influencer collab" — they want authentic, not sponsored

**Jargon level:** None — plain, warm, everyday language. Emojis welcome.

## Where They Hang Out

| Platform/Channel | How they use it | Why it matters |
|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Instagram | Discover new small brands, browse Reels, share finds in DMs | Primary discovery channel — visual-first, story-friendly |
| Etsy | Search for handmade gifts, read reviews, compare options | Primary purchase channel for handmade goods |
| TikTok | Watch making-of videos, discover trending products via "TikTok made me buy it" | Growing discovery channel, especially for the younger audience segment |
| Facebook Groups | Mom groups, handmade jewelry groups, local buy/sell communities | Word-of-mouth and recommendations happen here |
| Pinterest | Gift idea boards, jewelry styling inspiration | Long-tail discovery — pins stay relevant for months |

**People they trust:** Mom influencers (micro, not mega), small business spotlights, friends' recommendations in group chats, Etsy reviews

## Buying Triggers

What pushes them from "considering" to "buying":
- Upcoming birthday, holiday, or special occasion for a daughter/niece — they need the perfect gift NOW
- Seeing a making-of video or the twins' story on social media — emotional connection tips them over
- A friend recommends the brand or shares a purchase — social proof from someone they trust
- Custom color option — "I can get her favorite colors??" turns browsers into buyers
- The $1 charity donation — removes the last hesitation ("at least my money is going to a good cause too")

## Secondary Segments

### Segment: Teen Girls (8-15)
- **Who:** Girls who love trendy beaded jewelry, ocean aesthetics, and collecting bracelets to stack
- **Key difference from primary:** They're the wearers, not the buyers. They discover on TikTok and Instagram, then ask a parent to buy. Price-conscious but trend-driven.
- **Priority:** High — they drive demand even if they don't hold the credit card

### Segment: Self-Treating Women (20-35)
- **Who:** Young women buying for themselves — drawn to the aesthetic and the mission, not buying as a gift
- **Key difference from primary:** Buying for personal use, more style-focused, may lean toward the pearl + gold chain pieces rather than the colorful stacked bracelets
- **Priority:** Medium — growing segment as the brand expands its range
